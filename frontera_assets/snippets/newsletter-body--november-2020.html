Frontera on the Front Lines During Record Hurricane Season

Throughout 2020, TACC provided emergency access to Frontera to simulate and predict the storm surge incoming tropical storms would produce. Frontera enables researchers to run three scenarios in the time it previously took to run one, and to use larger, more resolved coastal meshes, providing better guidance to emergency managers and decision-makers.

New Collection of Stars, Not Born in Our Galaxy, Discovered in Milky Way

Caltech researchers combined massive cosmological simulations, observational data from the Gaia space observatory, and deep learning algorithms on Frontera to identify Nyx, a new collection of 250 stars that they believe are the remnant of a dwarf galaxy that merged with the Milky Way eons ago. It represents the first group of stars discovered in this way. The results were published in Nature Astronomy.

Supercomputers create world's most detailed simulations of tornadoes

Atmospheric scientist Leigh Orf is using Frontera to simulate the largest, most realistic supercell thunderstorms ever achieved. His research has identified several features that may be critical in determining whether a tornado will form, helping meteorologists to forecast tornadoes early and give more advance warning of a storm.

Texascale Days at TACC: Labor Day Edition

The Texascale Days event in September provided an opportunity for eight research groups to use large sections Frontera, from 100,000 processing cores to the entire system. Research topics ranged from COVID-19 simulations to experiments with new community HPC libraries.

Pulling the Plug on the Coronavirus Copy Machine

In May 2020, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration authorized the antiviral drug remdesivir for emergency treatment of COVID-19. The Cisneros research group at the University of North Texas used Frontera to investigate how remdesivir and other available drugs inhibit the coronavirus’ ability to replicate.

Mysterious Cellular Droplets Come into Focus

Research is shedding light on a type of membrane-less organelle within cells, known as biological condensates, that plays a role in DNA repair and aging. Using Frontera, biophysicists from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign explored one specific condensate, known as fused in sarcoma, that regulates gene expression.

Connect Your Research to the Cloud with Frontera

Frontera's design includes integration with cloud services, providing users with options for data storage and access to emerging computing technologies. Projects utilizing data of exceptional importance or looking to experiment with emerging computational capabilities (such as Tensor processors) can do so on Google, Microsoft, and Amazon clouds.

Accelerate your Work on Frontera with GPUs

Did you know that Frontera has two GPU-based subsystems available to all users with an allocation? Longhorn, an IBM POWER9-hosted system with 448 NVIDIA V100 GPUs, is part of the Frontera project and offers 3.5 petaflops of double-precision performance. A liquid submerged system with 360 NVIDIA Quadro RTX 5000 GPUs adds 7 petaflops of single-precision performance.
